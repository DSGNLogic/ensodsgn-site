About

What is the
MID Toolkit?

The Ministry of Intentional Design (MID) was created to help churches rethink ministry through the lens of empathy, emotional awareness, and transformational care. The Gospel does not change — but the way we design ministry experiences must respond to the full complexity of human experience.

This toolkit gives your team a complete framework and practical working tools to design ministry that helps people feel seen, supported, healed, and transformed.

01
The empathy-centered philosophy of Feel First — slowing down to understand before creating solutions.
02
Principles of human-centered design — research, listening, and co-creation with the people you serve.
03
The structure of design thinking — reimagined through the lens of ministry and spiritual care.
The Framework

5 Phases of Intentional Ministry Design

Phase 01
Empathize
Listen to Learn

Slow down to notice people deeply. Recognize grief, exhaustion, shame, and isolation before reaching for solutions. Listening sessions, community interviews, and emotional experience audits.

Phase 02
Understand
Applied Humility

Move empathy into clarity. Identify root tensions and deeper ministry challenges beneath surface-level symptoms like low attendance or volunteer burnout.

Phase 03
Design
Intentional Creation

Brainstorm, ideate, and prototype intentional ministry solutions. Create systems, environments, and experiences with purpose and care.

Phase 04
Heal
Impact Through Application

Implement ministry care through support, connection, restoration, and relational healing. Move from design into real community transformation.

Phase 05
Transformation
Ministry Outcome

Spiritual growth, relational connection, emotional healing, discipleship, belonging, and sustainable long-term impact across your entire ministry ecosystem.
